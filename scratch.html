About My Tennis Journey

Tennis has been a major part of my life. I spent years as a tennis instructor at Cliff Drysdale Tennis (Red Ledges) in Heber, Utah, coaching players of all ages and skill levels. Teaching the sport taught me patience, strategy, and a deep appreciation for the technical beauty of the game.

I've been recognized four times as an Academic All-State tennis player at WHS, and tennis remains one of my greatest passions alongside pickleball. This page is a celebration of everything I love about the sport.

The Grand Slams

Tennis's four major tournaments, each played on a unique surface:

  1. Australian Open (Hard Court)
    • Held in Melbourne, Australia every January
    • Played on DecoTurf hard courts
    • Known for extreme summer heat
  2. French Open / Roland Garros (Clay)
    • Held in Paris, France each May–June
    • The only major played on red clay
    • Nadal has won here a record 14 times
  3. Wimbledon (Grass)
    • The oldest tennis tournament in the world (1877)
    • Played on natural grass courts in London
    • Famous all-white clothing rule
  4. US Open (Hard Court)
    • Held in Flushing Meadows, New York each August–September
    • Played on Laykold hard courts (blue surface)
    • Known for the loudest, most energetic crowd

Tips from a Former Instructor

After coaching hundreds of players at Red Ledges, here are the fundamentals I always emphasized:

  • Footwork first. 80% of tennis is getting in position — the swing comes last.
  • Watch the ball all the way to contact. Most mishits are head-turning early.
  • Topspin is your friend. Heavy topspin gives you margin over the net and keeps balls in.
  • Serve placement > serve speed. A well-placed 100 mph serve beats a straight 130 mph every time.
  • Stay mentally calm. One bad game doesn't define a set. Reset and refocus.
